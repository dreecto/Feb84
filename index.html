The Problem

A $130 billion economy without purpose-built financial tools

Hispanic contractors are the fastest-growing segment in U.S. construction, yet they remain critically underserved by traditional financial institutions.

Limited Access to Capital

Traditional banks reject the majority of loan applications from Hispanic-owned construction businesses, citing thin credit files and non-traditional income documentation.

Complex Payment Workflows

Managing subcontractor payments, lien waivers, retainage, and progress billing across multiple projects — often with paper checks and spreadsheets.

Language Barriers

Most financial platforms offer limited or no Spanish-language support, creating friction and exclusion for the millions of contractors who prefer to operate bilingually.
